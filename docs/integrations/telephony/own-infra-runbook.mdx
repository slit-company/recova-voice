title: "Own-infrastructure telephony runbook"
description: "Operate supplier-independent Korean telephony without exposing carrier credentials to customer organizations."
---

## Prerequisites

- A Recova operator account with superadmin access.
- A customer organization selected for assignment.
- A published inbound workflow in the customer organization.
- A written internal change record for the planned validation window.
- For supplier-independent regression checks: local backend dependencies installed.
- For live validation attestation: supplier access for the SIP/070 trunk you intend to validate.

This runbook is for technical readiness. It does not decide legal, regulatory, or carrier-contract eligibility.

## Trust boundaries

- Customer organizations can bind assigned Recova-managed numbers to their own workflows, but they must not edit hidden Jambonz carrier credentials or provider configuration.
- Operator actions happen in **Telephony number inventory** under superadmin.
- Customer actions happen in **Assigned phone numbers** and remain bind/unbind only.
- Simulator or contract-fixture results are useful regression evidence, but they must not be counted as live SIP/070 readiness.

## Operator flow

1. Import Korean 070 inventory in `/superadmin/telephony-number-inventory`.
2. Reserve a number for the target organization before customer handoff.
3. Assign the number only after the target organization and workflow ownership are confirmed.
4. Before supplier access, run the Jambonz contract smoke check:

   ```bash
   PYTHONPATH=.:pipecat/src api/.venv/bin/python -m api.scripts.jambonz_contract_smoke
   ```

5. Verify the row status, backing configuration row, phone-number row, audit entries, and readiness metadata.
6. After a real SIP/070 route proof exists, click **Attest live** on the assigned inventory row and enter the real-route evidence id.
7. Quarantine a number immediately when supplier, routing, fraud, or quality concerns make it unsafe to use.
8. Retire a number when it must not return to customer use.

## Customer flow

1. Open `/telephony-numbers`.
2. Select an assigned number.
3. Choose an inbound workflow from the same-organization workflow picker.
4. Save the binding.
5. Confirm inbound behavior only after the live validation gate is approved.

The customer page intentionally does not expose provider credentials, Jambonz trunk settings, or raw carrier identifiers.

## Operational notes

- Use audit history for who changed inventory state and when.
- Treat readiness metadata as evidence pointers, not a legal approval.
- **Attest live** is an operator-only technical evidence write. It records a pointer to a real route proof; it is not legal, regulatory, or business launch approval.
- Do not route customer traffic through a number that is quarantined, retired, fixture-only, or missing live validation evidence.
