---
title: "SIP/070 cutover checklist"
description: "Checklist for moving Korean 070 traffic from preparation to controlled live validation."
---

## Prerequisites

- Supplier SIP/070 access is available and documented internally.
- Carrier credentials are stored only in operator-owned configuration.
- The customer organization has a published inbound workflow.
- A rollback owner and validation window are named in the change record.

## Before cutover

- Confirm the number is assigned to the correct organization.
- Confirm the customer workflow picker shows only same-organization workflows.
- Confirm the customer cannot edit hidden Jambonz credentials or provider configuration.
- Confirm the inventory row is not quarantined or retired.
- Confirm fixture and simulator runs are labeled as fixture evidence and not live readiness.
- Confirm no customer SIP/070 traffic is scheduled before supplier access and live gates are complete.

## Live validation gate

- Use only approved live-validation tooling or operator attestation for live readiness evidence.
- Record the validation source and evidence identifier.
- Attach the evidence to the inventory/readiness metadata without copying carrier secrets.
- Check CDR/status evidence for the intended provider, configuration, phone number, inventory id, and call attempt.

## Rollback

- Quarantine the inventory number if validation fails or produces ambiguous evidence.
- Disable the backing phone-number row before any customer traffic is allowed to continue.
- Clear the inbound workflow binding when routing safety is uncertain.
- Record the reason in audit history.

## Cutover complete

Cutover is technically complete only when the assigned inventory row, backing phone-number row, audit history, and live readiness evidence all point to the same organization and the number remains active and unquarantined.
